ICT - TVL SENIOR HIGHSCHOOL STRANDS AVAILABLE

Computer system servicing

The Computer System Servicing strand is a specialized course of study that provides students with in-depth knowledge and practical skills in maintaining and troubleshooting computer systems. This educational pathway is designed to equip learners with the technical expertise needed to support and optimize computer networks and infrastructure. Students enrolled in this strand typically delve into topics such as hardware and software diagnostics, system installation and configuration, network setup and administration, and cybersecurity measures. Practical hands-on training is often emphasized, allowing students to gain real-world experience in troubleshooting common computer issues and performing routine maintenance tasks. Upon completion of the strand, graduates are prepared to pursue careers as computer technicians, network administrators, or IT support specialists, contributing to the efficient functioning of organizations in various industries.

Home Economics

Home Economics is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on the management and economics of the home and community. It encompasses a wide range of topics, including nutrition and meal planning, family health, consumer education, personal finance, textiles and clothing, interior design, and human development. The goal of Home Economics is to improve the quality of life for individuals, families, and communities by teaching practical skills and fostering an understanding of how to manage resources effectively. It combines aspects of social science, physical science, and applied arts to provide a holistic approach to everyday living, empowering people to make informed decisions and lead sustainable, balanced lives.
