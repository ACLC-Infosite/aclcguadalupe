Home Economics (TVL)

Home Economics is an interdisciplinary field that focuses on the management and economics of the home and community. It encompasses a wide range of topics, including nutrition and meal planning, family health, consumer education, personal finance, textiles and clothing, interior design, and human development. The goal of Home Economics is to improve the quality of life for individuals, families, and communities by teaching practical skills and fostering an understanding of how to manage resources effectively. It combines aspects of social science, physical science, and applied arts to provide a holistic approach to everyday living, empowering people to make informed decisions and lead sustainable, balanced lives.

Programming (ICt)

The programming strand in senior high school is designed for students interested in software development and coding. It covers essential topics such as algorithms, data structures, programming languages (like Python, Java, or C++), and software development methodologies. Students learn to write, test, and debug code, as well as develop applications and websites. This strand emphasizes logical thinking, problem-solving, and project management skills. It prepares students for further studies in computer science or IT-related fields and equips them with the knowledge needed for careers in software engineering, web development, and other tech industries.

Computer systems services (ICt)

The CSS (Computer Systems Servicing) strand in senior high school focuses on teaching students how to set up, maintain, and repair computer systems and networks. Key topics include hardware and software installation, troubleshooting, network configuration, and cybersecurity basics. Students gain hands-on experience through practical exercises and projects. This strand equips students with technical skills needed for IT support roles and prepares them for further education or certifications in computer systems and networking. It aims to develop problem-solving abilities and a strong foundation in computer technology.

Animation (ICt)

The animation strand in senior high school is designed for students interested in pursuing careers in animation and digital arts. It covers subjects like drawing, character design, 2D and 3D animation, storytelling, and the use of animation software. Students learn both the artistic and technical aspects of animation, including motion graphics and visual effects. This strand helps develop creativity, technical skills, and an understanding of the animation industry. It prepares students for further studies or careers in animation, gaming, film, and other multimedia fields.
